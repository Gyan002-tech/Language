Lesson 0003 · Spoken Fluency & Articulation

Why meetings felt harder

You weren't just learning PREP — you were learning PREP and professional wording, at the same time. This lesson removes the second load.

What actually happened

Both settings being tough after one lesson is completely normal — that's a brand-new structure, and effort right now is what makes it stick later. But meetings being specifically harder than casual conversation has a clear cause: in a meeting, you're solving two problems in one breath — what to say (the PREP structure) and how to say it professionally (word choice). In casual conversation, the wording is already automatic for you, so only the structure is new. In a meeting, both are new at once.

The fix isn't more meeting examples — it's removing one of the two loads. If the professional phrasing becomes a reflex, all that's left to think about while speaking is the content, same as in conversation.

The phrase bank

Four rows, one per PREP beat. Every phrase in a row is interchangeable — pick whichever comes to mind fastest.

Recognize the beat

Same four questions, testing the phrase bank rather than content — this is what needs to become automatic.

1. "My recommendation is we renew with the current vendor."
2. "That's driven by their support response times being twice as fast."
3. "Case in point: last month's outage took four hours to resolve with the other vendor."
4. "Net-net, staying with the current vendor is the safer call."

Two more meeting scenarios — phrase bank allowed

Keep the phrase bank open in another tab if you want. The goal this time isn't generating wording from scratch — it's plugging content into a ready-made frame.

Scenario A: Should we renew the vendor contract?

Scenario B: Your manager asks you to justify a budget increase request

Scenario A:
"My recommendation is we renew with the current vendor. That's driven by their support response times being twice as fast as the alternative. Case in point: last month's outage took four hours to resolve with the other vendor, versus under one hour with ours. Net-net, staying put is the safer call."

Scenario B:
"I'd go with approving the increase. The key factor is we're currently understaffed for the Q3 workload. For example, two people are already covering what used to be a three-person rotation. Which is why I think the increase is justified, not optional."

Say it out loud

Say both answers out loud once. Then try one of them again without looking at the phrase bank — that second pass, without the scaffold, is the real test of whether it's starting to stick.
