# Refined diagnosis: passive vocabulary is strong, active/productive vocabulary is the actual gap

Satyam clarified that his earlier "word selection doubt" (lesson 0004) understates the real problem: he
understands words fine receptively (in context, when someone else uses them), but doesn't feel confident
enough in a word's *exact* meaning/nuance to deploy it himself when constructing sentences. This is a classic
passive-to-active vocabulary transfer gap, not primarily a collocation or perfectionism issue — those still
apply, but sit on top of this deeper gap.

He is independently reading *Word Power Made Easy* (Norman Lewis) — a well-regarded, etymology-based
vocabulary book organized around Greek/Latin root words and their derivative families. This is a strong fit
for closing exactly this gap (systematic, meaning-precise vocabulary building, not rote lists), and should be
folded into the course as the primary vehicle for module 2 rather than treated as a side project. See
[[MISSION.md]] and refines [[0003-phrase-bank-worked-and-priorities-named]].

**Implication:** the next major body of work is extracting root-word/derivative notes from this book,
chapter by chapter, into `reference/` as a glossary — and future lessons should draw vocabulary practice
from that glossary rather than inventing unrelated word lists.
