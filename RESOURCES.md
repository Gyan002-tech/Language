# Spoken English Fluency & Articulation — Resources

## Knowledge

- [Shadowing Technique — Dr. Alexander Argüelles](https://www.alexanderarguelles.com/about/)
  Real-time listen-and-repeat drilling against native audio. Use for: closing the "translate-then-speak" lag that causes choppy pauses; building rhythm and automaticity.
- [PREP Framework (impromptu speaking) — Toastmasters International](https://www.toastmasters.org/magazine/magazine-issues/2023/sept/impromptu-speaking)
  Point → Reason → Example → Point structure for organizing an answer on the spot. Use for: meetings, Q&A, and everyday conversation where you need to sound coherent with zero prep time.
- [_Talk Like TED_ — Carmine Gallo](https://www.carminegallo.com/talk-like-ted/)
  Patterns distilled from top TED speakers. Use for: presentation structure and persuasive storytelling delivery.
- ["Increase Your Speaking Vocabulary" — Annemarie Fowler, Speak Confident English](https://www.speakconfidentenglish.com/increase-speaking-vocabulary/)
  Targets word-retrieval *speed*, not new-word memorization. Use for: the "I know the word but it won't come out in time" bottleneck.
- [Rachel's English](https://rachelsenglish.com/)
  The best-regarded course/channel for American English mouth-position and sound clarity for non-native speakers. Use for: pronunciation clarity work.
- [Toulmin Model of Argument — via UMW Speaking Program](https://academics.umw.edu/speaking/resources/handouts/toulmin-argument-model/)
  Claim / Grounds / Warrant / Rebuttal, explained practically rather than academically. Use for: structuring a persuasive case in debate or workplace disagreement.
- [BBC Learning English — Tim's Pronunciation Workshop (YouTube playlist)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLcetZ6gSk96-ayXj5thbTpbh2vHWpP08o)
  Short, credible BBC-produced videos on connected speech and intonation. Use for: clarity beyond individual sounds — how words link in real speech.

- [Cambridge Dictionary "About Words" — collocation mistakes](https://dictionaryblog.cambridge.org/2018/10/17/making-an-effort-and-telling-a-joke-avoiding-common-errors-with-collocations/)
  Corpus-backed post on classic verb+noun collocation errors. Use for: "is this word grammatically correct here" doubt — usually a collocation issue, not true grammar. Pair with the [Cambridge collocation lookup](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/collocation/english/) for any specific word.
- [YouGlish](https://youglish.com)
  Free, no login: type a word/phrase, hear it spoken in-context by native speakers in real video clips. Use for: "do native speakers actually say this" doubt.
- [Ludwig.guru](https://ludwig.guru)
  Free tier (5 searches/day): paste a phrase, see real matching sentences from trusted publications. Use for: checking if a written/spoken phrase sounds native before committing to it.
- [Dörnyei & Scott (1997), "Communication Strategies in a Second Language," _Language Learning_ 47:173–210](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/0023-8333.51997005)
  Peer-reviewed SLA taxonomy naming "approximation" (a close-enough word to fill a lexical gap) as a legitimate strategy. Use for: the citable basis for "good-enough word now, refine later."
- [Comma Gets a Cure — IDEA diagnostic passage](https://www.dialectsarchive.com/comma-gets-a-cure)
  Standard short story built on Wells' lexical sets, purpose-designed to surface a broad spread of English vowel/consonant contrasts. Use for: self-diagnosing which specific sounds are actually the problem, without needing a human listener.
- [Shadowing + self-record comparison method](https://englisheducationlab.net/shadowing-technique-what-it-is-and-why-every-esl-learner-should-use-it/)
  Mimic a native clip in real time, record yourself, play back against the original. Use for: spotting your own mismatches directly.
- [ELSA Speak](https://elsaspeak.com)
  Freemium app; AI scores each phoneme against native models, color-coded per sound. Use for: pinpointing specific problem vowels/consonants/stress without a human listener.

- [_Word Power Made Easy_ — Norman Lewis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word_Power_Made_Easy)
  Classic vocabulary-building book organized around Greek/Latin root words and their derivative families, rather than rote word lists. Use for: closing the passive-to-active vocabulary gap — knowing a word's precise meaning well enough to produce it, not just recognize it. Source of the workspace's root-word glossary in `reference/`.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [Toastmasters International](https://www.toastmasters.org/find-a-club)
  Global network of in-person and virtual clubs (90+ years running) for structured speech practice with real-time peer feedback. Use for: presentations, impromptu speaking (Table Topics), debate-style exchanges.
- [r/EnglishLearning](https://www.reddit.com/r/EnglishLearning/)
  ~600K+ members, active text-based Q&A and pronunciation feedback threads. Use for: troubleshooting a specific phrase or sound; less useful for live speaking practice.
- [iTalki](https://www.italki.com/)
  10,000+ tutors including low-cost "Community Tutor" conversation-only sessions. Use for: live 1:1 spoken practice with a real interlocutor and direct feedback.
- [English Speaking Practice (Discord)](https://discord.com/invite/english-speaking-practice-998792086197383168)
  ~100K+ members, live voice channels for informal group conversation. Use for: casual, free, on-demand speaking practice — less structured than Toastmasters or iTalki.
