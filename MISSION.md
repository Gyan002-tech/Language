# Mission: Spoken English Fluency & Articulation

## Why
Satyam understands English fluently but struggles to *produce* it well — sentences come out clunky, the right word doesn't arrive in time, fluency breaks down into pauses, and pronunciation sometimes gets in the way of being understood. The goal is to close the gap between comprehension and expression: to speak in a way that matches how well he already understands, across work meetings, everyday conversation, debates, and storytelling.

## Success looks like
- Speaks in complete, well-formed sentences on the fly, without backtracking mid-sentence to fix grammar
- Retrieves the precise word for an idea quickly, instead of settling for a vaguer word or stalling
- Speaks in longer stretches with fewer filler-driven pauses (natural fluency, not memorized scripts)
- Is consistently understood without needing to repeat or rephrase due to pronunciation
- Can hold his own in a persuasive/debate exchange — structuring an argument out loud, not just understanding one
- Can narrate an event or explain an idea as a coherent story, not a list of disconnected facts

## Constraints
- ~10-15 minutes per day, most days — lessons must be short and self-contained, not require a big block of time
- Speaking practice needs to work without a live conversation partner (solo practice), since sessions are asynchronous with the teacher

## Out of scope
- Nothing explicitly excluded yet — Satyam chose to keep grammar drills, pronunciation, and vocabulary all on the table rather than narrow early. Revisit this once a few sessions reveal where the real bottleneck is.
